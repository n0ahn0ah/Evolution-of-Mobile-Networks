• 3G, an abbreviaton for 3ʳᵈ generation, was launched commercially in mid-2001. The first pre-commercial 3G network was launched in Japan in 1998 under the name, FOMA, standing for the Freedom of Mobile multimedia Access. The first commercially available 3G mobile network was released by NTT DoCoMo in Japan on October 1ˢᵗ, 2001. 3G was not provided with a clear definition of what the rate of upload/download is, although ITU expected to have a minimum data rate of 2Mbit/s for walking and stationary users and 348 kbit/s in moving vehicles. 3G provides much more security than it's previous iteration, 2G. The UE (User Equipment) authenticates the network to ensure it is a verified 3G network and not an impersonation of one.
