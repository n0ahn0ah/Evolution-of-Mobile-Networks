• 1G

    • Launched in Tokyo, Japan 1979, the first commercially automated cell network was released to the metropolitan area of Tokyo by NTT. In under 5 years, this network had expanded across the entirety of Japan's population as well as becoming the first nationwide 1G network. The Nordic Mobile Telephone system was launched simultaneously in Sweden, Finland, Norway and Denmark. This was the first mobile network to include international roaming. Just 2 years later, in 1983, the Chicago-based company Ameritech released the first 1G network in the USA. The mobile phone released with 1G in the US was the Motorola DynaTAC 8000X, a phone that was priced at $3,995 in 1983. Other countries such as the UK, Mexico and Canada followed suit in releasing 1G mobile phones in the early to mid-1980's. 1G is a reference to the first generation of mobile telecommunications. These are the standards for analog telecommunications produced in the 1980's and remained until the digital upgrade to 2G was introduced in 1991. The largest difference between 1G and 2G is that 2G audio transmissions were completely digital whereas 1G was analog.
