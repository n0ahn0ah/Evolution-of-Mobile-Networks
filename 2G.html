• 2G

    • 2G, which stands for second-generation cellular network, was launched in 1991 on the GSM standard by Radiolinja in Finland. 2G is the successor to 1G, featuring digitally encrypted phone conversations, more users allowed per frequency band due to more efficient use of the radio frequency spectrum. 2G was also the first network to include data services for mobile, allowing users to send SMS text messages. These technologies that 2G enabled include networks providing picture, text and MMS (multimedia messages) messages. 2G compared to our current standards is rather sluggish as opposed to 4G/4GLTE. 2G has a speed range of 64Kbps to 256Kbps whereas 4G has a speed range of 100 Mbps to 1 Gbps. That's 10^6 of what 2G was able to offer at it's highest performance. For the time however, 2G was revolutionary in what it gave way to and in it's performance. Although 2G is generally less used currently due to better technology being available in most places, many still use devices running off 2G like the UK who widely uses 2G for dumbphones and vehicle tracking devices. Despite it's old technology, 2G still remains a useful service for locations where the price to run newer services is too high.
