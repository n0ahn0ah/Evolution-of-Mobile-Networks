• 4G stands for 4th Generation. 4G is the fourth evolution of cellular networks, preceding 5G and succeeding 3G. 4GLTE was released commercially in 2009 in Norway, Oslo and Stockholm, Sweden. It has been debated as to if the first generation releases should be considered 4GLTE. Often times, 3GPP LTE is branded as 4GLTE however it does not comply fully with the IMT-Advanced requirements. IMT-Advanced requirements for 4G include a basis on an all-IP packet switched network, up to 1 Gbit/s for low mobility and up to 100 Mbit/s for high mobility, ability to dynamically use and share network resources to allow more concurrent users per cell. Peak download and upload speeds for 4GLTE-Advanced are 1000 Mbit/s and 500 Mbit/s, respectively. In the US, large carrier Sprint shut down their WiMAX network on March 31, 2016 and T-Mobile, who merged with Sprint in April 2020, plans to shut down their 4GLTE network on June 30, 2022.
